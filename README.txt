Why I Had to Build the "Elementor is Shite Fix" (And Why You Need It Too)
Stop me if you’ve heard this one: You’re in the middle of a high-speed build. You drag a widget into a container. You’re ready to drag the next one. But instead of letting you work, Elementor decides to force-switch your sidebar to the "Style" tab.

You click back to the widget list. You scroll. You drag. It switches again.

Repeat 500 times a day.

For those of us building complex digital marketplaces and high-performance sites, Elementor’s UI isn’t just "opinionated"—it’s a bottleneck. It’s built for visual tweakers, not for developers who need to move at scale.

The Problem: Creative vs. Functional Workflow
Elementor assumes you want to style every single element the moment it hits the canvas. But when you’re building out a localized service directory or a request-and-bid flow, you need to lay down the structure first. You need 10 widgets on the page in 10 seconds, not a forced trip to the padding and margin settings every time you breathe.

The Solution: The "Elementor is Shite Fix" (EISF)
I got tired of the friction, so I built a fix. It’s a lightweight WordPress plugin that does what the core team should have done years ago: It puts the developer back in control.

What does it actually do?

The Widget Lock: It aggressively snaps the sidebar back to the widget list after every drop. No more auto-switching.

The Dual-Panel Hack: It injects a second, floating, searchable widget library into the editor. You can keep your "styling" panel open on the left and your "widget drawer" open on the right.

One-Click Injection: No more drag-and-drop fatigue. Click a widget in the secondary panel, and it drops instantly into your layout.

The God-Mode Toggle: Need to return to "Normal Mode"? One hotkey (Ctrl + Shift + Z) or one toggle button restores standard behavior instantly.

Why I’m Sharing This
I’m currently deep in the trenches building localized marketplaces (shoutout to my fellow Auckland devs!), and this simple JS/PHP injection has literally cut my layout time in half.

If you’re a dev who feels like you’re fighting your page builder more than the code itself, this is for you. It’s zero-bloat, it’s free, and it’s born out of pure, unadulterated workflow frustration.

Is Elementor perfect? Far from it. Is it usable now? With this fix, absolutely.
